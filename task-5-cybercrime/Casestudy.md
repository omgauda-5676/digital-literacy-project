#  Real Talk: The "QR Code" UPI Scam
**A Case Study by:** Om Prakash Gauda  
**Your Campus Digital Ambassador** ---

##  What is this actually?
Imagine you're selling an old textbook or a pair of headphones on a campus group or OLX. Someone messages you instantly, agrees to your price, and seems like the perfect buyer. But instead of sending you money, they send you a **QR code**. 

This isn't just a technical glitch; it’s a **social engineering trap**. It plays on your excitement to make a quick sale and your misunderstanding of how UPI actually works.

---

##  How it goes down (The Step-by-Step)

1. **The Fast Buyer:** You get a DM. The person is super friendly and says, *"I'm busy at work, let me send you an advance via my Business UPI so the item is blocked for me."*
2. **The Bait:** They send a QR code over WhatsApp. They tell you, *"Just scan this in GPay or PhonePe, and the 2,000 rupees will be added to your bank account immediately."*
3. **The Rush:** They stay on the call with you, saying things like, *"Bro, hurry up, the link expires in 30 seconds."* This pressure stops you from thinking clearly.
4. **The Critical Mistake:** You scan the code. Your app pops up with a screen asking for your **UPI PIN**.
5. **The "Poof" Moment:** You enter your PIN thinking you're "receiving" the money. Instead, you hear a *ding*—but it’s an SMS saying your account has been **debited**. The "buyer" immediately blocks you and disappears.



---

##  Who are they looking for?
Honestly? **Us.** Students who are active on marketplaces, looking for internships, or waiting for scholarship refunds. We use UPI for everything from chai to tuition, and scammers know that we sometimes move too fast for our own good.

---

##  What happens next?
* **Empty Pockets:** Losing ₹2,000 might not seem like much to some, but for a student, that’s a month’s worth of canteen meals or travel. 
* **The "Authorized" Deadlock:** Because **you** entered your secret PIN, the bank considers the transaction "authorized." This makes it incredibly hard to get your money back.
* **The Stress:** There's a lot of "How could I be so stupid?" feeling involved. It ruins your week and makes you paranoid about using digital payments again.
