
## Overview
As students navigating a digital-first academic environment, our online safety is paramount. This checklist provides 8 actionable steps to secure your digital life, specifically tailored for the Indian college context.

---

##  Prevention Checklist

1. **The UPI Golden Rule:** Never enter your **UPI PIN** to receive money. If someone sends you a QR code and claims it is for a "refund" or "payment" that requires a PIN, it is a scam.
2. **Verify Official Apps:** Only download banking and UPI apps (PhonePe, Google Pay, BHIM) from the official Play Store or App Store. Avoid "Lite" or "Modded" versions shared on Telegram or WhatsApp.
3. **Secure Your Credentials:** Use a unique, strong password for your university portal (V-TOP) and primary email. Enable **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** using an authenticator app rather than just SMS.
4. **Beware of "Job" Scams:** If you receive a WhatsApp or Telegram message offering "Work from Home" tasks that involve liking YouTube videos for money, block the sender. These often lead to sophisticated task-based financial frauds.
5. **Public Wi-Fi Safety:** Avoid accessing your bank accounts or sensitive student data while connected to free public Wi-Fi at cafes or railway stations. If necessary, always use a **VPN**.
6. **Check the Link (URL):** Before entering credentials on any site, ensure the URL is correct (e.g., `vitbhopal.ac.in` and not `vitbhopall.com`). Scammers often use "typosquatting" to steal login details.
7. **Social Media Privacy:** Set your personal Instagram and Facebook profiles to "Private." Avoid sharing photos of your **College ID card** or **Hall Ticket**, as these contain sensitive data like your registration number and DOB.
8. **Think Before You Click:** Be skeptical of "Urgent" emails or SMS alerts claiming your bank account or SIM card will be blocked. These are phishing attempts designed to create panic.


##  How to Report Cybercrime
If you or someone you know has been a victim of cybercrime, immediate reporting is the best way to freeze fraudulent transactions and seek justice.

* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Toll-Free Helpline:** **1930** (Available 24/7)
